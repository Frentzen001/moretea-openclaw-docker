Garage@EEE Facilities and Equipment

Keywords: facilities equipment tools 3D printer laser cutter PCB milling machine drill press acrylic bender soldering oscilloscope workbench booking form project storage meeting room

Garage@EEE has a well-equipped space for student projects covering fabrication, electronics, and general making. Here is an overview of what is available and how to use it.

---

Fabrication and Making Equipment

The 3D printers can turn digital designs into physical prototypes and models. To use a 3D printer, members must first submit a 3D print request form through the Garage@EEE Toolbox, followed by a filament submission form. Both steps are needed before printing begins.

The laser cutter can engrave and cut materials like wood and acrylic, and is ideal for custom signage, enclosures, or intricate shapes. Members need to sign the laser cutter key in and out when using it. The key check in/out form is in the Toolbox.

The PCB milling machine lets you prototype and produce circuit board designs quickly without waiting for external fabrication. It is useful for one-off or low-volume PCB work.

For general making and woodwork, Garage has a fully equipped workbench with drills, saw clamps, and a variety of hand tools. The drill press handles precision drilling through wood and plastic. The acrylic bender lets you curve acrylic sheets to make enclosures and display stands. A heat gun is also available for heat-forming and shrink-wrapping.

---

Electronics Equipment

The electronics workstation is the primary area for circuit work and testing. It has soldering stations for all skill levels, from complete beginners to experienced makers. There are also rework stations for desoldering and fine component work.

Test equipment includes oscilloscopes, waveform generators, DC power supplies, multimeters, and breadboards for prototyping. Backup units for most of this equipment are located in the Heavy Machines Room.

---

Project Storage and Workspace

Garage has project storage shelves where members can keep materials and components organised between sessions. There is also a bookable working area for focused project sessions, and a meeting room with a TV screen for sharing presentations, doing design reviews, or running workshops.

---

Booking and Reporting Forms

Members access all forms through the Garage@EEE Toolbox linktree. To find it, open the Garage@EEE family Telegram group and look in the GARAGE101 topic.

Key forms available:
- Seat booking: Reserve a working area for your project session.
- Item loan form: Borrow equipment or items from Garage@EEE.
- 3D print request: Submit a job to have something 3D printed (do this first).
- 3D filament submission: Submit a filament request after your print request (second step).
- Laser cutter key check in/out: Sign the laser cutter key in or out.
- Purchase Approval Form: Required before purchasing materials or equipment for a project.
- Fault Reporting and Suggestions: Report broken equipment or suggest improvements.
- TR/LT Booking Form: Book a tutorial room or lecture theatre for events.
- Bus Booking for Events: Book transport for Garage events.
- Garage tour request: For groups or external visitors who want a guided tour.
- Sports and games loan (Garage@EEE Connect): Borrow sports and games equipment.
