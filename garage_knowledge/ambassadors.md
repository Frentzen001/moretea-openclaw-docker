Garage@EEE Ambassador Portfolios

Keywords: ambassador portfolios roles welfare outreach branding marketing operations business development startup training workshops events leadership design communications inventory stakeholders LinkedIn mentorship

The Ambassador Track is one of the two main ways to be part of Garage@EEE. Ambassadors take on active leadership roles and run real initiatives for the Garage community and beyond. There are six portfolios, each with a distinct focus.

---

Welfare and Outreach

Welfare and Outreach is responsible for community bonding and engagement. They organise events and activities for Garage members to bring the team together. They also run CIP (Community Involvement Programme) projects to inspire young engineers from the wider community. Additionally, they host delegations and guest groups — including international visitors and school tours — who come to see Garage@EEE and learn from what students are building here.

Keywords: welfare outreach bonding events CIP community involvement delegations tours guests members

---

Branding and Marketing

Branding and Marketing maintains and develops Garage@EEE's public image. They create posters, graphics, social media content, and visual collateral to attract potential new ambassadors, sponsors, and partners. They develop strong communication and design skills, and are the face of Garage in the way it presents itself to the world.

Keywords: branding marketing design posters graphics social media content communications sponsors visual identity image

---

Operations

Operations keeps the makerspace running day to day. They are responsible for operating and maintaining all equipment, managing the inventory of tools and materials, and providing logistical support for all events and initiatives run by Garage. If something is broken, needs restocking, or needs to be set up for an event, Operations handles it.

Keywords: operations equipment maintenance inventory logistics events support day-to-day running makerspace

---

Business Development

Business Development manages Garage's external relationships and partnerships. They correspond with industry stakeholders, scout potential contacts and partners on LinkedIn, write professional emails, and actively build Garage's network with companies, alumni, and other organisations. This portfolio is ideal for students who want to develop professional communication and networking skills.

Keywords: business development stakeholders partnerships LinkedIn networking professional emails external relationships industry alumni

---

Startup Development

Startup Development nurtures innovation and entrepreneurship among Garage members. They guide aspiring tinkerers and innovators who are taking their first steps with a project idea, offering encouragement, structure, and direction. They work alongside experienced mentors who help bring early-stage projects to life. This portfolio bridges the gap between having an idea and actually building something.

Keywords: startup development innovation entrepreneurship mentorship early-stage projects ideas guide support tinkerers innovators

---

Training and Development

Training and Development builds technical skills and knowledge within the Garage community. Members of this portfolio become trainers and facilitators for workshops on topics like electronics, programming, fabrication, and design. They push themselves to continuously learn new skills so they can teach others effectively. They also serve as makeathon mentors, supporting participants during events like Escendo and STARTathon.

Keywords: training development workshops technical skills facilitators trainers electronics programming fabrication makeathon mentors learning
