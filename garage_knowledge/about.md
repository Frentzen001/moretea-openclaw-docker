Garage@EEE — What It Is and How to Join

Keywords: Garage EEE NTU makerspace student-led engineering innovation entrepreneurship join membership tracks ambassador innovator tinkering innotrack launchpad funding apply

Garage@EEE is a student-led makerspace in the School of Electrical and Electronic Engineering (EEE) at Nanyang Technological University (NTU). It gives students the environment, tools, equipment, and funding they need to develop ideas, build real things, and grow as engineers and innovators — all alongside their academic studies. Garage has strong industry connections and a large alumni network, so students do not just build projects here, they build careers and companies.

The core mission is to encourage innovation and cultivate entrepreneurship among EEE students, and to instil a genuine passion for engineering, building, and ideation in the wider community. Garage is not just a space — it is a community of makers, thinkers, and people who want to build things that matter.

---

The Two Main Tracks

There are two ways to be part of Garage@EEE: the Ambassador Track and the Innovator Track.

The Ambassador Track gives students a platform to lead and shape the Garage community. Ambassadors propose and run initiatives across six portfolios: Welfare and Outreach, Branding and Marketing, Operations, Business Development, Startup Development, and Training and Development. It is a great fit for students who want to build leadership, communication, and organisational skills while making a real impact.

The Innovator Track is for students who want to bring ideas to life. Students can come in with a startup concept, a project idea, or just curiosity, and Garage will provide guidance, funding, and access to professional facilities. There are three programmes: Tinkering Projects, Innotrack, and Launchpad. You do not need a polished idea to apply — you can start from scratch and develop something here.

---

Who Can Join

Garage@EEE is open to NTU EEE students. For the Innovator Track, at least one team member must be an NTU EEE student. Students from other schools or faculties may join as part of a team.

---

How to Find Out More or Get Involved

Visitors interested in joining should speak to the team at the front desk. They can point you to the right programme or portfolio based on your interests. Information about the Tinkering Projects application form can be found in the Garage@EEE Toolbox (accessible via the GARAGE101 topic in the Garage@EEE family Telegram group).
