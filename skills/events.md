Garage@EEE Events

Garage@EEE runs several events through the year for students and the wider community.

STARTathon is an internal makeathon-style robotics competition where students design, build and compete with custom robots. The theme changes each year — past editions had battlebots and soccerbots, and this year is a line-following robot challenge. It is a great entry point for freshmen to get into hands-on robotics.

GEEEnius is Garage's internal orientation camp for members. It brings the team together to build relationships, understand each other's strengths and set the foundation for collaborative projects through the year.

Enitio is a Freshmen Orientation Programme run by Garage@EEE under the Faculty of EEE. The flagship activity is a large-scale Treasure Hunt that mixes laser tag and scavenger hunt elements across campus using a self-built game controller called the Wanderers. It helps freshmen settle into university life and make friends while doing something hands-on and exciting.

The Innovation Festival is Garage's anniversary showcase and networking event. Industry professionals, faculty, staff, alumni and community partners come together to see top student projects. Outstanding teams get to present their ideas to experienced professionals, making it a great networking opportunity.

Escendo is Garage's flagship makeathon camp. Participants build a prototype around a given theme, learn technical skills from workshops, and pitch their work to judges at the end. It brings together students, industry professionals and innovators to collaborate intensively.

IdeasJam is a nationwide ideathon organised by Garage@EEE. Participants come up with innovative solutions for a specific theme, supported by workshops and mentorship to sharpen their ideas and pitching skills. It is open to students across Singapore, not just NTU.

Makerspace Friendlies is a joint event with SUTD Makerspace. The 2026 edition, called Rapidfire Rover, runs over two days. Day one is an autonomous maze challenge at NTU where your rover must independently navigate and map an unknown layout. Day two is an arena battle at SUTD where you pilot your rover and use collected power cores as ammunition to score against other teams.
