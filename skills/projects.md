Garage@EEE Student Projects

These are the current innovator projects running at Garage@EEE. They span a wide range of domains including robotics, AI, hardware, health tech, and consumer apps. Most teams are small and student-led, building real working prototypes with Garage's support.

All project openings are currently marked as Not Recruiting. Students interested in joining a team should check back or speak to the team at the front for the latest status on openings.

---

HeatSig

HeatSig incorporates thermal imaging sensors into existing building lighting infrastructure to create smart lighting that also tracks footfall and human traffic data. It can help building managers understand how spaces are used and optimise energy consumption.

---

Play Makers

Play Makers is an escape room experience that combines a cryptex-style mechanical puzzle with a laser mirror maze. Players must reflect a laser beam off a sequence of mirrors onto a sensor to unlock the room and win. It is a fully self-contained, resettable game experience.

---

Learnr

Learnr is a platform focused on making lifelong learning more accessible and engaging across a wide range of topics. It aims to build a community where learners can discover new areas of interest, find resources, and grow together.

---

FRAM3D

FRAM3D creates personalised 3D hologram souvenirs by scanning people or objects in a custom-built 360-degree photobooth and producing a portable holographic memento. It combines photogrammetry, 3D printing, and holographic display technology.

---

Team ReBina

Team ReBina is building a smart recycling bin that uses computer vision to automatically identify and sort waste into the correct categories, reducing contamination in recyclables — a common problem with manual recycling.

---

ESCAPEE

ESCAPEE is a remote-controlled escape room experience where players navigate puzzles and challenges using a live video feed from an RC car. The player controls the car remotely and must interact with the environment to solve the puzzles and escape.

---

Team Rexus

Team Rexus is building a Mars Rover to compete in the prestigious University Rover Challenge (URC). The team is learning and applying skills across CAD design, 3D printing, ROS (Robot Operating System), PCB design, and AI to build a capable rover.

---

Garage 2.0 (BEHeRe)

BEHeRe is a blockchain-based electronic health record system designed to make healthcare data in Singapore more secure, interoperable, and patient-controlled. It aims to give patients ownership over their own medical records and make it easier to share them with authorised providers.

---

REYSS

REYSS is a fall detection system that uses an infrared (IR) camera to detect when elderly residents fall in their homes, and automatically sends an alert notification to caregivers. It is designed to improve elderly safety and response times without requiring wearable devices.

---

Wunderkids

Wunderkids is building an automated commode potty washer to reduce the manual cleaning workload for nurses and caregivers in medical facilities, and to lower their exposure to bacteria and biohazards during routine cleaning tasks.

---

Quick Checkout

Quick Checkout is a mobile app that lets shoppers scan items as they put them in their basket and pay directly through the app, eliminating the need to queue at a cashier or a self-checkout kiosk. It is aimed at retail environments looking to reduce checkout friction.

---

DIP E026 — Escape Room Design

DIP E026 integrates technology into escape room hardware design. The team builds feedback-enabled props and puzzle mechanisms that are easy to reset and operate with minimal manpower, making it practical to run commercial escape rooms efficiently.
