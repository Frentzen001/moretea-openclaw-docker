Garage@EEE Facilities

Garage@EEE has a range of equipment and tools for student projects. The 3D printers can turn digital designs into physical prototypes and models. The laser cutter can engrave and cut materials like wood and acrylic for custom signage or intricate shapes. The PCB milling machine lets you prototype and produce circuit board designs quickly without waiting for external fabrication.

For general making, there is a fully equipped workbench with drills, saw clamp and other hand tools. The drill press handles precision drilling through wood and plastic. The acrylic bender lets you curve acrylic sheets for enclosures and displays.

For electronics work, there is a soldering station for all levels, from beginners to experts. The electronics workstation has oscilloscopes, power supplies and everything needed for hands-on circuit projects.

Garage@EEE also has project storage shelves for keeping materials and project components organised. There is a working area that students can book for project work. There is also a meeting room complete with a TV for sharing presentations and ideas.

Member forms and booking tools:
All the forms and booking tools for members are collected in the Garage@EEE toolbox linktree. To find it, open the Garage@EEE family groupchat on Telegram and look in the GARAGE101 topic.

Available forms and what they are for:
- Seat booking: Reserve a working area inside Garage@EEE for your project session.
- Item loan form: Borrow equipment or items from Garage@EEE.
- 3D print request: Submit a job to have something 3D printed by the team. Members should do this first.
- 3D filament submission: Submit a filament request after your print request has been made. This is the second step in the 3D printing process.
- Laser cutter key check in/out: Sign a laser cutter key in or out when you use it.
